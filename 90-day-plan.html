90-Day Objective

Establish a lightweight product operating rhythm that improves clarity, priority, delivery flow, release readiness, adoption, and learning - without adding heavy process overhead.

Days 0-30
Read the demand system

  • Meet executive sponsors, business owners, BI, data engineering, platform, governance, and representative users.
  • Map active work, intake channels, decisions, owners, blockers, dependencies, timelines, and escalation paths.
  • Review Power BI/Fabric architecture, deployment practices, security, access, semantic models, quality controls, and ownership with technical leads.
  • Baseline decision latency, blocked time, rework, release defects, and retained-use signals where data exists.
  • Select one decision-critical workstream for a bounded proof.

Days 31-60
Install product judgment

  • Launch a concise weekly product review using Build / Validate / Sequence / Stop dispositions.
  • Define minimum problem, outcome, owner, evidence, delivery, and adoption fields for active work.
  • Clarify release readiness across data quality, validation, access, usability, support, and user acceptance.
  • Unblock the selected workstream and communicate tradeoffs in plain language.
  • Create an adoption review date and named business owner before release.

Days 61-90
Prove and scale

  • Release or materially advance the selected product with documented quality, access, usability, and ownership evidence.
  • Measure retained use and operating outcome using agreed units and baseline.
  • Retire, narrow, or redirect at least one low-value or ambiguous request.
  • Publish reusable product patterns and recurring causes of delay or rework.
  • Present the next-quarter portfolio thesis, capability priorities, and recommended capacity allocation.

Operating Cadence

Weekly Product Review

Active work, decision changes, blockers, tradeoffs, release risks, stakeholder commitments, and next action.

Release Readiness Review

Data quality, validation, access, usability, ownership, support path, and adoption mechanism.

Outcome Review

Retained use, business signal, friction, feedback, and Expand / Revise / Retire decision.

Monthly Portfolio Thesis

What is being funded, sequenced, stopped, learned, and why.

30-Day Discovery Questions

Business

  • Which decisions have the highest consequence?
  • Where is trust weakest?
  • What recurring surprises create escalation?

Product portfolio

  • How is work currently requested and prioritized?
  • What work is repeatedly reopened?
  • Which products are used versus merely available?

Platform

  • Where do Fabric, Power BI, data engineering, and source-system responsibilities begin and end?
  • Which definitions and semantic models are authoritative?

Delivery

  • What blocks work most often?
  • How are quality, access, validation, and release readiness checked?
  • How are tradeoffs communicated?

Adoption

  • Who owns behavior change?
  • How is retained use observed?
  • What user feedback reaches the team?

Learning

  • Which patterns should be reused?
  • Which recurring causes should be removed?
  • What should stop?

Risk Controls

  • Process overhead: use one visible review and a minimum viable record, not a new bureaucracy.
  • Platform alignment: partner with Fabric and Power BI specialists to connect architecture, governance, and delivery standards to portfolio priorities and business outcomes.
  • Stakeholder fatigue: ask for only the decisions and commitments required to move work.
  • Output bias: schedule adoption and outcome review before release.
  • Unsupported value claims: define units, baseline, source, and owner before presenting impact.

Day-90 Readout

A credible day-90 review should show the portfolio logic, one workstream’s before-and-after flow, release and adoption evidence, work stopped or redirected, recurring causes identified, platform and product capability priorities, and the next-quarter choices. It should not rely on an invented maturity score or unsupported percentage.
