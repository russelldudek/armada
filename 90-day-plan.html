Read the system. Stabilize flow. Prove the loop.

The first 90 days should produce a credible operating picture, a clearer portfolio, and one end-to-end proof that connects request qualification, delivery, release readiness, adoption, and learning.

Days 1–30 · Read and baseline

  • Map request channels, active work, recurring stakeholders, informal decision rights, dependencies, release checks, and adoption practices.
  • Shadow BI, Data Engineering, and business users through real work—not only status meetings.
  • Review current Power BI/Fabric architecture, operating conventions, access model, deployment path, and support boundaries with technical owners.
  • Select one bounded workstream as the platform-ramp and operating-model proof.
  • Establish a no-surprises update rhythm for sponsors and active stakeholders.

Outputs: current-state portfolio map; stakeholder topology; blocker taxonomy; baseline measurement plan; first proof workstream.

Days 31–60 · Install visibility

  • Introduce a minimal intake contract centered on decision, owner, value, readiness, and adoption.
  • Publish one portfolio view showing disposition, owner, next decision, blocker, release risk, and intended outcome.
  • Define product-type release checklists for reports, reusable datasets, automations, and AI-enabled workflows.
  • Clarify escalation boundaries and decision rights across business, BI, Data Engineering, access/security, and governance.
  • Run the selected workstream through the Outcome Cross-Dock.

Outputs: intake/disposition standard; active portfolio view; release contracts; decision-rights map; adoption plan.

Operating principle: do not launch a “new process” campaign. Improve the work visibly, borrow the team’s existing language where possible, and add only the minimum structure required to make decisions earlier and handoffs cleaner.

Days 61–90 · Close the loop

Release one priority product

  • Clear quality, validation, access, usability, support, and adoption conditions.
  • Make the human owner and escalation path explicit.
  • Communicate what changed, why, and what evidence will determine the next decision.

Receive the outcome

  • Observe actual use in the workflow.
  • Capture friction and workarounds without blaming users.
  • Compare intended decision behavior with observed behavior.
  • Decide: standardize, scale, revise, support differently, hold, or retire.

Institutionalize only what earned it

  • Promote reusable intake fields, release checks, patterns, or definitions only when the proof supports them.
  • Retain exceptions where the business context genuinely differs.
  • Publish a concise learning note and update the portfolio.

90-day executive readout

  • Portfolio clarity and decisions made
  • Delivery flow and blocker pattern
  • Release-quality findings
  • Adoption evidence and user friction
  • What to scale, change, stop, or investigate next

Day-90 success is not “the process is installed.” It is that ARMADA can point to one important decision product and see the entire chain: why it was prioritized, how it moved, what cleared release, who adopted it, what evidence returned, and what the team decided next.
