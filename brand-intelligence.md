## Identity assets
- Official ARMADA footer identity: `https://www.armada.net/wp-content/themes/amd2024/public/images/logo-footer.3a20a9.svg`
- The publication workflow retrieves the official SVG directly from ARMADA's current website and commits it locally as `assets/brand/armada-logo-official.svg`.
- Use: nominative identification only, paired with an explicit independent-candidate qualifier.
- Visible treatment: the footer-format mark is placed on a restrained navy field so its official light wordmark remains legible on both site and document surfaces.
- Alteration policy: the mark is not traced, recolored, distorted, animated, cropped, or merged into candidate artwork.

## Typography evidence and decision
ARMADA’s current public site uses a bold, clean, high-impact sans-serif character, with large compressed headlines, uppercase navigation, and strong white-space contrast. An authoritative public font specification was not located. The campaign uses locally available Lato as a licensed structural substitute and reproduces the public character through weight, scale, tracking, and density. No proprietary webfont file is copied.

## Photography, interface, and environmental cues
- Official visual language: large typography, high-contrast white/dark fields, bright multicolor identity accents, logistics/supply-chain imagery, line/grid systems, and interface-like operating readouts.
- Candidate execution: original cross-dock geometry, transfer lanes, disposition doors, and adoption-receipt readouts. No proprietary PRO® interface is copied or fabricated.
- Official photography is not used; the campaign avoids implying that Russell owns ARMADA products, facilities, customers, or outcomes.

## Motion character
- ARMADA’s public message centers on movement from visibility to action.
- Candidate-original motion translates that idea into an analytics-work transfer: one decision-product load moves from inbound demand through qualification and release toward disposition and adoption evidence.
- Reduced-motion mode resolves the load to a complete readable state.

## Artifact application
- Website: full identity palette, locally committed official ARMADA SVG, cross-dock metaphor, motion, interaction.
- Resume and cover letter: restrained blue rules, identity lockup, Lato hierarchy, minimal multicolor accents.
- Brief, plan, and review artifact: same document header, line grammar, evidence tables, and cross-dock lanes.

## Independent-candidate distinction
Every surface identifies the work as an independent candidate vision or candidate material by Russell Dudek. No endorsement, employment, sponsorship, or official authorship is implied.
