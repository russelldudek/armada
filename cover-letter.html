ARMADA’s Manager Data Products role is not really about managing a queue of reports. It is about deciding which data work deserves scarce attention, converting ambiguous needs into executable direction, keeping BI and data engineering delivery moving, and making sure released products change decisions and work.

That mandate closely matches how I operate. At Vape-Jet, I work directly across production, quality, purchasing, support, customer success, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, dashboards, and AI-assisted workflows. The work is practical: clarify the problem, expose dependencies, prioritize by consequence, create visible operating mechanisms, and follow delivery through adoption and learning. Through DudeWorth, I have also developed product and AI opportunity frameworks around value, data readiness, human authority, governance, evaluation, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value.

I would bring ARMADA a lightweight data-product operating rhythm: make the decision behind each request explicit; assess outcome, evidence, delivery, and adoption readiness; choose a clear Build, Validate, Sequence, or Stop disposition; and review retained use after release. That approach supports the job description’s bias toward action without turning the team into a process bureaucracy.

ARMADA's Microsoft analytics environment is a strong fit for how I create value. I bring hands-on fluency across reporting, data-bearing operating systems, telemetry, automation, AI workflows, quality, and cross-functional delivery, together with the product discipline to turn those capabilities into prioritized, validated, adopted solutions. I would work closely with BI and data engineering leaders from day one to accelerate the highest-value Power BI and Fabric opportunities, strengthen release and adoption practices, and build reusable operating patterns. The result is accountable product leadership that keeps technology anchored to business decisions and measurable operating outcomes.

The candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/armada/ makes that reasoning visible through an interactive Outcome Routing Table, a 90-day entry plan, and a role-specific review worksheet. It is an independent work product based on the public job description and ARMADA’s public strategy, not a claim about undisclosed internal processes.

I would value the opportunity to discuss which business decisions most need better data-product support, where delivery currently stalls, and what evidence would define a successful first 90 days.
