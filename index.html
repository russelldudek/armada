The operating model

Make product judgment visible.

Expose the logic behind Build, Validate, Sequence, and Stop decisions so tradeoffs are defensible and stakeholders know what happens next.

1

Name the decision

Clarify the problem, user, owner, and consequence before discussing a report or dataset.

2

Test the evidence

Check definitions, data quality, access, validation, and useful proof.

3

Commit the path

Choose the smallest responsible disposition: build, validate, sequence, or stop.

4

Review retained use

Measure whether the product changed work, then expand, revise, or retire it.

Outcome scorecard

Measure the product, the flow, and the behavior.

Every measure needs an agreed unit, baseline, owner, and review cadence.

V

Value evidence

Decision improved, service protected, cost avoided, or risk reduced.

F

Flow

Decision latency, blocked time, cycle time, handoff delay, and rework.

R

Release trust

Definition coverage, validation, access readiness, defects, and exceptions.

U

Retained use

Repeat users, workflow penetration, decision usage, and friction.

L

Learning

Feedback-to-change time, reusable patterns, and recurring causes removed.

First 90 days

Earn trust through one visible operating rhythm.

Days 0-30

Read the demand system

  • Map stakeholders, decisions, active work, blockers, and intake paths.
  • Review the Power BI/Fabric environment, definitions, access, release controls, and ownership with technical leads.
  • Baseline portfolio flow and select one decision-critical workstream.
  • Establish a concise weekly product review.
Days 31-60

Install product judgment

  • Introduce the Outcome Routing Table and explicit dispositions.
  • Define release-readiness and adoption-review criteria.
  • Unblock the selected workstream and clarify tradeoffs.
  • Document reusable patterns and recurring causes.
Days 61-90

Prove and scale

  • Release or materially advance the selected product with quality, access, and usability evidence.
  • Measure retained use and operating impact.
  • Retire or redirect low-value work.
  • Publish the next-quarter portfolio thesis, capability priorities, and capacity choices.

Executive interview questions

Questions that expose the real mandate.

  1. Which three business decisions most need better data-product support this year?
  2. Where does active work currently stall: problem definition, data readiness, delivery, release, or adoption?
  3. What work should this manager stop or redirect, not merely accelerate?
  4. How are Power BI and Fabric responsibilities divided across product, architecture, engineering, and governance?
  5. Which stakeholder groups trust current reporting least, and why?
  6. What is the boundary between hands-on analysis, product management, and team coordination?
  7. What does retained adoption look like for reports, datasets, automation, and AI-assisted products?
  8. What evidence would define a successful first 90 days?
