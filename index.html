The operating model

Make product judgment visible.

A lightweight product system should expose the logic behind Build, Validate, Sequence, and Stop decisions. That makes tradeoffs easier to defend and gives stakeholders a practical view of what happens next.

1

Name the decision

Clarify the business problem, user, owner, and consequence before discussing a report or dataset.

2

Test the evidence

Check definitions, data quality, access, validation, and what would constitute useful proof.

3

Commit the path

Choose the smallest responsible disposition: build now, validate first, sequence dependencies, or stop.

4

Review retained use

Measure whether the product changed work, then expand, revise, or retire it.

Outcome scorecard

Measure the product, the flow, and the behavior.

No unsupported maturity scores. Each measure should have a defined unit, baseline, owner, and review cadence established with ARMADA.

V

Value evidence

Named operating outcome, decision improved, cost avoided, service protected, or risk reduced.

F

Flow

Decision latency, blocked time, cycle time, handoff delay, and avoidable rework.

R

Release trust

Definition coverage, validation completion, access readiness, defects, and exceptions.

U

Retained use

Repeat users, workflow penetration, decision usage, abandonment, and user friction.

L

Learning

Time from feedback to change, reusable patterns, retired work, and recurring causes removed.

First 90 days

Earn trust through one visible operating rhythm.

Days 0-30

Read the demand system

  • Map stakeholders, decisions, active work, blockers, and existing intake paths.
  • Review Power BI/Fabric environment, release controls, definitions, access, and ownership with technical leads.
  • Baseline portfolio flow and select one decision-critical workstream.
  • Establish a concise weekly product review.
Days 31-60

Install product judgment

  • Introduce the Outcome Routing Table and explicit Build / Validate / Sequence / Stop dispositions.
  • Define release-readiness and adoption-review criteria.
  • Unblock the selected workstream and clarify stakeholder tradeoffs.
  • Document reusable patterns and recurring failure causes.
Days 61-90

Prove and scale

  • Release or materially advance the selected product with quality, access, and usability evidence.
  • Measure retained use and operating impact with agreed units.
  • Retire or redirect low-value work.
  • Publish the next-quarter portfolio thesis and capability gaps.

Executive interview questions

Questions that expose the real mandate.

  1. Which three business decisions most need better data-product support this year?
  2. Where does active work currently stall: problem definition, data readiness, delivery capacity, release, or adoption?
  3. What work would you like this manager to stop or redirect, not merely accelerate?
  4. How are Power BI and Fabric responsibilities divided across product judgment, architecture, engineering, and governance?
  5. Which stakeholder groups trust current reporting least, and what created that gap?
  6. What is the expected boundary between hands-on analysis, product management, and team coordination?
  7. What does retained adoption look like for a report, dataset, automation, or AI-assisted product here?
  8. After 90 days, what evidence would make you say this hire is reducing surprises and rework?
