Executive Thesis

Route data work to outcomes. ARMADA’s Manager Data Products should make product judgment visible: clarify the decision behind each request, test evidence and delivery readiness, choose a defensible disposition, and review retained use after release.

Actual mandate

Turn reporting, datasets, automation, and AI-assisted demand into a focused portfolio that moves through delivery and produces adopted business outcomes.

Company moment

ARMADA publicly describes “intelligence that acts,” a data and AI-driven PRO platform, and a 2026 integration of Armada, Sunset, and ATEC under one brand. Strong internal product discipline is a plausible enabler of that external promise.

Central contradiction

Increase speed and stakeholder responsiveness without becoming a passive request desk or adding heavy process overhead.

White-space thesis

A lightweight Outcome Routing Table can connect business consequence, evidence readiness, delivery path, and adoption ownership to explicit Build, Validate, Sequence, or Stop decisions.

Operating Tensions

  • Demand vs. judgment: stakeholders need support, but the product manager must form and defend opinions on what not to build.
  • Speed vs. evidence: progress over perfection cannot become release without data quality, validation, access, and usability.
  • Release vs. use: output is incomplete until the product changes a decision or workflow.
  • Platform vs. problem: Power BI and Fabric matter, but the business outcome should determine the product.

Decision Architecture

Build when outcome, evidence, delivery confidence, and adoption ownership align. Validate when one assumption needs bounded proof. Sequence when dependencies or capacity require explicit order. Stop when value or decision clarity does not justify effort.

Stakeholder Topology

Executive sponsors

Set enterprise outcomes, resolve cross-functional conflicts, and decide where capacity should be concentrated.

Business stakeholders

Own decisions, definitions, workflow outcomes, adoption, and the consequence of inaction.

BI / Data Engineering

Own technical design, feasibility, data quality implementation, platform patterns, deployment, and support boundaries.

Data platform / governance

Own architecture, security, access, lineage, standards, and reusable controls.

End users

Provide usability evidence, workflow reality, retained-use signals, and improvement feedback.

Product manager

Owns problem clarity, priority, tradeoffs, product direction, release readiness, stakeholder communication, adoption review, and disposition changes.

Balanced Success Measures

  • Value evidence: the operating outcome or decision improved, using agreed units and a documented baseline.
  • Flow: decision latency, blocked time, cycle time, handoff delay, and rework.
  • Release trust: definition, validation, access, usability, and exception readiness.
  • Retained use: repeat usage, workflow penetration, decision usage, abandonment, and friction.
  • Learning: feedback-to-change time, recurring causes removed, reusable patterns, and work retired.

Change Burden

Business leaders must own decisions and outcomes rather than outsource them to BI. Technical teams need transparent priority and acceptance logic. Users must participate in validation and behavior change. The product manager must push back without creating distance, and make priority changes explainable.

Compunetics: technical-business translation, quality, controls, and high-reliability execution. Cardinal / ZeusVu: distributed digital workflows and governed technology operations.
