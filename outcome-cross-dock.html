Outcome Routing Review

Use this one-page review to turn a vague request into an explicit product decision. Values and examples must be established with ARMADA; the sample row below is illustrative.

FieldQuestionIllustrative example
Problem / decisionWhat decision or workflow needs to improve?Leaders need one trusted view of service exceptions requiring cross-functional action.
Owner / usersWho owns the outcome, and who will use the product?Named business owner; operations and commercial leaders as repeat users.
Business consequenceWhat happens if the problem remains unresolved?Slower intervention, repeated reconciliation, inconsistent escalation.
Evidence readinessAre definitions, sources, quality, access, and validation sufficient?Definitions aligned; two source-quality exceptions require bounded validation.
Delivery pathWhat is the smallest responsible product and dependency set?Initial Power BI view using governed semantic model; no AI feature in first release.
Release readinessHow will quality, usability, access, ownership, and support be verified?User validation, access test, exception review, owner signoff, support handoff.
Adoption mechanismWhat behavior changes, and who reinforces it?Weekly operating review uses the product as the source for exception decisions.
Outcome evidenceWhich unit and baseline will be used?Decision latency and reconciliation effort, baselined before release.
DispositionBuild, Validate, Sequence, or Stop - and why?VALIDATE: prove the two source exceptions and user workflow before broad build.
Next reviewWho decides what happens next, and when?Business owner + product + BI lead after bounded validation.

Disposition Rules

Build: sufficient outcome, evidence, delivery, and adoption ownership. Validate: one critical assumption needs proof. Sequence: dependency or capacity must be resolved first. Stop: unclear decision or insufficient value.
